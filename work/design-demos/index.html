Pointwell — design exploration

Six UI concepts, each attacking a different friction in a habit / reward app. Built as live, self-contained demos — tap, drag, type, watch them move. Always-dark, calm, native-feeling.

↓ Open any one (best at a narrow / phone width — each is a 390px screen).

01
🃏
Swipe Stack
Speed · logging
Your whole day as a card stack — swipe right to log, left to skip, up to enter an amount. The daily check-in in ten seconds instead of tap-tap-tap.
Open demo →
02
◍
Day Ring
Glanceability
Your entire day as one radial gauge — every habit is an arc that fills with progress, one number in the middle. No list to scroll; the whole day in a glance.

03
📈
Momentum
Motivation framing
Reward showing up, not just big days. A consistency score + a contribution heatmap that turns "I keep skipping" into a pattern you can see and beat.

⌘
Command Bar
Input
One smart bar. Type the way you think — "ran 30 min", "slept 8h", "8000 steps" — watch it parse live and log anywhere. Power-user speed, zero menus.

05
🫙
Reward Vault
Purpose
It's a reward system, but points are abstract. Pick a real reward and watch a vessel fill toward it — effort you can see, with a satisfying unlock.
